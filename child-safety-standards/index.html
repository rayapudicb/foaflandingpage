Child safety standards and CSAE policy

Nyr has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, sextortion, trafficking, or any behavior that sexually exploits, abuses, or endangers a child.

What is prohibited on Nyr

Content

  • Any CSAM or sexualized imagery involving a minor.
  • Requests, offers, chats, or uploads that sexualize, exploit, or endanger children.
  • Content that facilitates trafficking, coercion, or exploitation of a child.

Behavior

  • Grooming, sextortion, or predatory contact with a minor.
  • Attempts to solicit sexual content from or involving a child.
  • Using Nyr to organize, promote, or enable child exploitation.

How Nyr responds

1. Review

We review reports, preserve relevant evidence as needed, and prioritize potential child safety risks.

2. Enforce

We may remove content, restrict features, suspend accounts, or permanently ban users who violate these standards.

3. Report

Where required by law, we report confirmed CSAM or related child exploitation matters to NCMEC or the relevant regional authority.

Reporting a concern

If you believe a user, post, message, image, or video may involve child exploitation or abuse, report it through the in-app reporting flow when available, or contact our child safety point of contact below.

  • Include the account, post, or conversation involved.
  • Do not reshare harmful content beyond what is needed for reporting.
  • If a child is in immediate danger, contact local law enforcement immediately.

Policy scope

These standards apply to Nyr, a Gigasys Technologies Inc. app, and cover user-generated content, profiles, messages, uploads, and any interaction enabled through the service.
